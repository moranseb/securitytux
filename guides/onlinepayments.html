Online Payments

Click through the sections below to learn about how to stay safe when shopping online.

There are a few ways your credit card information can be stolen. First, your device that you use to shop online can be compromised. Credential and information stealing malware can steal your credit card number if you have it saved in your browser, or if you make a purchase while infected. Make sure whatever device you use to shop online is secure and free of malware. Also, don't visit sketchy sites from the same device you use to shop. Use a Virtual Machine if you absolutely have to visit a site you don't trust, but it's better to avoid them all together.

Second, your credit card infomation can be involved in a breach. If a website you've shopped at is breached, your payment information could be compromised. To avoid this, use virtual cards from privacy.com. See more on this in the next section. These breaches happen most often to smaller, outdated websites. You are very unlikely to have your credit card compromised because a big company you shop at like Amazon was breached. Because of this, avoid entering your payment information into sites you don't trust. Some smaller sites may use third party payment processors. This is usually safe.

Third, an account where your credit card information is saved could be breached. If your Amazon account is breach, for example because you use a password you've used elsewhere, an attacker may be able to steal your payment information from your account. To avoid this, make sure any accounts where you save payment information are secured with strong passwords and 2FA.

Last, your payment information could be stolen by a credit card skimmer. These are physical devices that criminals attach to payment terminals, especially at gas stations. Before swiping your card, you can pull hard on the payment terminal to make sure there is no skimmer attached. If a payment terminal looks strange or different from other ones around it, avoid using it. Skimmers are placed over top of existing card terminals, so watch out of particularly bulky or pertruding terminals. For more infomration on spotting skimmers, check out this article: https://www.howtogeek.com/193958/atm-skimmers-explained-how-to-protect-your-atm-card/.

Virtual cards, such as those offered by privacy.com, allow you to generate multiple payment cards when shopping online. There are various controls you can use when using these cards. You can create "One time use" cards that close and decline all subsequent transactions after being used, or you can have a card that will lock to the first merchant it is used at. This means if you make a card for Amazon shopping, after it is used at Amazon, it cannot be used anywhere else. You can also set spend limits per week, month, or year on each card. If this limit is exceeded, the card will decline. These virtual cards help eliminate much of the risk from online shopping. If you card is breached, hackers will not be able to use it at other merchants. If it was a one time card, they won't be able to use it at all, and if the card is still usable, but has a limit, they will only be able to use it at the merchant the card is locked to, and will not be able to exceed the limit placed on the card. Additionally, these cards help lessen the inconvenience of having payment information stolen. Instead of cancelling you credit card and having to update your billing information everywhere, you can cancel just the one virtual card that was breached.
